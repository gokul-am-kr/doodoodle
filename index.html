Course curriculum

6 phases.
20 sessions.

30 minutes per session. Two tracks — Kids (5–14) & Adults (18+). Built on permission, not perfection.

01
Foundation

Objects from Shapes

Learn to see any object as a combination of simple shapes. Camera, flower pot, bicycle, plant — drawn step by step from reference images, then freely.

4 sessions · 2 hours
02
Colour

Colour & Markers

Introduction to markers and colour palettes. Learn colour blocking, basic shading, and how to make your doodles vibrant and alive.

2 sessions · 1 hour
03
Expression

Faces & Expressions

Draw a full range of emotions — happy, sad, surprised, sleepy, cheeky and more. Create your own personal expression reference chart to keep and use.

3 sessions · 1.5 hours
04
Character

Objects Come Alive

Place expressions on objects and give them real personality. A grumpy cactus. A curious book. A cheerful succulent. Design your own cast of character objects.

3 sessions · 1.5 hours
05
Scene

Doodle Scenes & Projects

Learn composition and layout basics, then plan and draw your own complete doodle scene. Includes a 3-panel story strip and a fully coloured themed artwork.

4 sessions · 2 hours
06
Mindful

Zentangle & Showcase

Discover meditative zentangle patterns — fill backgrounds, decorate objects, and add intricate borders. End with a Grand Finale Showcase of your best work, celebrated.

4 sessions · 2 hours

Your instructor

Hi, I'm
Gokul.

Creator of the Doo Doodle Method

I'm an artist, doodle enthusiast, and the creator of Doo Doodle — a drawing method built on one simple idea: creativity is not a talent you're born with. It's a habit you build, one line at a time. My journey didn't start in an art school. It started at a desk, burned out from a career in software development, staring at screens for hours on end.

One evening, I picked up a pen and started scribbling — and that small, quiet moment changed everything. Today I conduct workshops and work with students in small, personal sessions — helping beginners, professionals, and curious minds discover the joy of putting pen to paper. No judgement. No pressure. Just the freedom to create.
